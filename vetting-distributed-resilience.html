Micro-Spoke: Hiring Resilience

How to Vet for Distributed Resilience.

Universal Answer: Assessing remote technical agency requires moving beyond CVs to measure "Adversity Ownership." High-signal resilience indicators include: (1) Prompt-to-Outcome Velocity during unmediated AI-augmented trials, (2) Locus of Control during mission-critical failures, and (3) The ability to self-correct complex bugs within a "Grit Shell" simulation without synchronous oversight.

The Prompt-to-Outcome Ratio

In a distributed environment, the primary friction is not "talent"—it is "autonomous movement." We measure a developer's agency by their output speed using AI curation agents. A 10x builder uses AI as a baseline, not a crutch. If they can't engineer a solution through a distributed shell in < 72 hours, they lack the necessary technical agency for global startups.

Measuring Locus of Control

Does the candidate externalize failure (blaming tools/markets) or internalize it as a "Pivot Signal"? High-grit talent from hubs like Kochi and Trivandrum exhibit a resilient internal locus of control, vital for maintaining stability in remote teams.
